# legal — owned sections

## 9.1

Not applicable. The APS Markdown Conversion Service is an ephemeral document conversion utility that does not make, automate, or materially influence administrative actions or decisions that have legal or significant effects on individuals, groups, or communities. It functions solely as a formatting tool for public servants to convert diverse web and document formats into standardized Markdown, and does not perform any decision-making or profiling functions.

## 9.2

Not applicable. Because the system does not perform, automate, or materially influence administrative actions affecting rights or interests (as detailed in section 9.1), there are no administrative actions of this nature to challenge, rendering a challenge process not applicable.

## 10.2

No. No accessibility or human rights reviews have been conducted to ensure that the Markdown outputs comply with human rights obligations, such as those under the Disability Discrimination Act 1992 (Cth) (HTI State of AI Governance, p. 48, 68). Although the system is a deterministic parser, the threshold assessment notes potential risks of stripping accessibility features (such as alt text or semantic headers) during conversion, which could disadvantage individuals with disabilities (HTI State of AI Governance, p. 48).

*Citations: [HTI State of AI Governance, p. 48], [HTI State of AI Governance, p. 68]*
