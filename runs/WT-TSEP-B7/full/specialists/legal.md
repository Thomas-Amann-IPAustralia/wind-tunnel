## 9.1

Not applicable. The C&D Claim Evaluator is a public-facing chatbot designed strictly to provide informational support and educational resources to small business owners evaluating cease and desist letters. It does not make, or materially influence, formal administrative decisions or administrative actions that have a legal or significant effect on individuals or organisations.

## 9.2

Not applicable. Because the chatbot is an informational tool and does not make or materially influence administrative actions or decisions with legal or significant effects, there are no administrative actions arising from this system to challenge. However, users can provide feedback through standard business.gov feedback mechanisms if they dispute the chatbot's informational outputs.

*Citations: [ADM Better Practice Guide, p. 57]*

## 11.1

No. The use-case outline does not specify that mechanisms are currently in place to ensure responsibility and accountability by the agency and relevant individuals for the AI system and its outcomes. To comply with the Australian Government's Policy for the responsible use of AI in government, the agency must designate an accountable official to oversee policy implementation and designate an accountable use case owner within 12 months of the policy taking effect. Additionally, because this is a high-risk use case, the agency must report the use case to the Digital Transformation Agency (DTA) and govern it through a designated board or senior executive.

*Citations: [AI Use Policy v2.0, p. 11], [AI Use Policy v2.0, p. 16]*

## 12.2

Yes. 

- 12.2.1: Yes, the need for legal advice has been identified. Given that the chatbot analyzes unstructured legal documents and provides assessments on the validity of legal claims, there is a high risk of severe financial and legal harm if a user acts on incorrect AI advice. External and internal legal advice is essential to review the correctness of the legal interpretations programmed into the AI, draft legally robust disclaimers, and ensure compliance with administrative law, privacy, and liability frameworks.

- 12.2.2: The legal advice is not yet stored as the project is in the conceptual/assessment phase and formal legal advice has not yet been commissioned. Once obtained, the legal advice will be stored in the agency's secure electronic document and records management system (EDRMS) under the project file for the C&D Claim Evaluator (Run ID: WT-TSEP-B7).

*Citations: [ADM Better Practice Guide, p. 13], [ADM Better Practice Guide, p. 49], [New Machinery of Government, p. 19]*
