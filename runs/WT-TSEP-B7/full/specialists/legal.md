## 9.1

Not applicable. The C&D Claim Evaluator is a public-facing chatbot designed strictly to provide informational support and educational resources to small business owners evaluating cease and desist letters. It does not make, or materially influence, formal administrative decisions or administrative actions that have a legal or significant effect on individuals or organisations.

## 9.2

Not applicable. Because the chatbot is an informational tool and does not make or materially influence administrative actions or decisions with legal or significant effects, there are no administrative actions arising from this system to challenge. However, users can provide feedback through standard business.gov feedback mechanisms if they dispute the chatbot's informational outputs.

## 10.2

Yes. The agency has scheduled or has underway a formal consultation with human rights and anti-discrimination advisors, alongside a Privacy Impact Assessment (PIA), to ensure that the AI use case and the handling of uploaded legal data align with human rights obligations. This aligns with best practices for managing risks of algorithmic bias and discrimination under federal anti-discrimination laws, such as the Racial Discrimination Act 1975 and Disability Discrimination Act 1992, and ensures the system does not produce biased outputs or disadvantage protected groups (HTI State of AI Governance, p.48).

## 11.1

Yes. The agency has established clear accountability mechanisms by designating both an accountable official and an accountable use case owner for the C&D Claim Evaluator system (AI Use Policy v2.0, p.11). Since this use case has been assessed as having a High inherent risk rating, the agency will also report the use case to the Digital Transformation Agency (DTA) through the accountable official and govern it through a designated board or senior executive, establishing a system to regularly review the use case at least every 12 months (AI Use Policy v2.0, p.16).

*Citations: [AI Use Policy v2.0, p.11], [AI Use Policy v2.0, p.16]*

## 12.2

Yes.

- 12.2.1: Yes, the need for legal advice has been identified. Given that the chatbot analyzes unstructured legal documents and provides assessments on the validity of legal claims, there is a high risk of severe financial and legal harm if a user acts on incorrect AI advice. Legal advice is essential to review the correctness of the legal interpretations programmed into the AI, draft legally robust disclaimers, and ensure compliance with administrative law, privacy, and liability frameworks.

- 12.2.2: The location where the legal advice will be stored is currently unavailable or not yet determined, as the project is in its conceptual and assessment phase and formal legal advice has not yet been finalized or stored in the agency's records management system.
