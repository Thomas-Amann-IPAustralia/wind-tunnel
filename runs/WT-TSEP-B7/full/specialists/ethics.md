## 8.2

Yes. The AI chatbot will be hosted publicly on business.gov, and the interface will feature a clear, persistent disclaimer outlining the scope and goals of the tool, specifically clarifying that the output is informational and not a substitute for professional legal counsel. This aligns with transparency recommendations to publish clear information about the system's scope, capabilities, and limitations to set appropriate user expectations.

*Citations: [VAISS, s 10], [NAIC RAI Tools, p.33]*

## 8.4

Yes.

- 8.4.1: Yes, users will be clearly informed that they are interacting with an AI system through a persistent disclaimer on the web-based chat interface on business.gov, which clarifies that the chatbot's outputs are informational and AI-generated.
- 8.4.2: Yes, a non-AI alternative is available. The project team has considered a static, comprehensive 'self-help' guide or checklist on business.gov as a non-AI alternative. To comply with accessibility and inclusion standards, this static guide should be made readily accessible to users who do not wish to interact with the AI chatbot.

*Citations: [NAIC RAI Tools, p.33], [AI Ethics Principles, §**AI Ethics Principle 6: Transparency and explainability**], [ADM Better Practice Guide, p.32]*

## 8.5

Yes. The chatbot will provide explanations by highlighting specific paragraphs in the uploaded cease and desist letters that appear to lack a legal basis and linking them directly to relevant business.gov guidance articles. This allows users to understand the specific factors and regulatory frameworks underpinning the AI's insights, aligning with the principle of providing clear, non-technical explanations that help users understand the logic behind AI-generated recommendations.

*Citations: [AHRC HRT Report, §Non-technical reasons or explanation], [NAIC RAI Tools, p.33]*
