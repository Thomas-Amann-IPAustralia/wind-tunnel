## 6.1

Yes. The chosen data—consisting of internal performance review documents, goal-tracking data, and manager-logged feedback notes—is directly relevant to synthesizing a staff member's performance over an assessment period. However, to ensure suitability and mitigate risks of bias or inaccuracy, the data must be checked for coverage, completeness, and potential systemic bias. Specifically, unstructured feedback logs and meeting transcripts can contain gendered, neurodivergent, or historical biases that the AI model might replicate or amplify if not carefully managed. Therefore, while the data types are functionally suitable for performance drafting, ongoing data quality checks and human-in-the-loop verification are required to ensure the inputs are accurate, complete, and unbiased.

## 8.3

Yes. The agency will implement processes to maintain comprehensive records throughout the AI lifecycle in accordance with National Archives of Australia (NAA) guidance. This includes capturing AI-generated draft summaries, final approved evaluations, and associated metadata into the agency's approved records management system. To ensure transparency and traceability, the agency will make risk-based decisions to retain the prompts, inputs, and source data (such as manager notes and feedback logs) that contributed to specific outputs. Furthermore, system design, development, testing, and operational policies will be documented and retained to demonstrate how the system achieves its outputs.

*Citations: [NAA AI Records Guidance, §Managing AI technologies incorporated into business systems], [NAA AI Records Guidance, §Disposal of generative AI assistant prompts and inputs], [NAA AI Records Guidance, §Example: AI technologies incorporated into a regulatory compliance assessment business system]*
