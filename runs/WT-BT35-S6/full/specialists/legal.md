## 9.1

No. The current project outline does not specify any mechanism or requirement to notify staff members that their performance evaluations or summaries were drafted or materially influenced by the AI system. Under administrative law and best practice, individuals should be informed when automated or AI systems are used to make decisions or take actions that materially impact their rights or interests (ADM Better Practice Guide, p.42).

*Citations: [ADM Better Practice Guide, p.42]*

## 9.2

No. There is no timely or accessible process currently detailed in the project outline to challenge the administrative actions or evaluations influenced by the AI system. While standard agency HR grievance processes may exist, no AI-specific contestability or review mechanisms have been designed to allow staff to dispute the AI's role or outputs in their evaluations, which is a key requirement for responsible AI use (ADM Better Practice Guide, p.10, p.57).

*Citations: [ADM Better Practice Guide, p.10], [ADM Better Practice Guide, p.57]*

## 10.2

No. The project outline and threshold assessment do not indicate that the project team has consulted an appropriate source of advice or conducted an assessment to ensure alignment with human rights obligations. Engaging legal and policy experts to ensure compliance with human rights and privacy law is essential when designing systems that affect individual livelihoods and careers (ADM Better Practice Guide, p.13, p.49).

*Citations: [ADM Better Practice Guide, p.13], [ADM Better Practice Guide, p.49]*
