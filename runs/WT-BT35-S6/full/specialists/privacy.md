## 7.1

No. At this stage of the project's development, we cannot be fully satisfied that the collection, use, and disclosure of personal information complies with the Australian Privacy Principles (APPs). The proposed 'Manager Performance Assessment Assistant' will process highly sensitive HR data, including performance reviews, goal-tracking data, and potentially un-anonymized meeting transcripts or feedback logs (Threshold Assessment §3.5). To ensure compliance, several key areas must be addressed through a formal Privacy Impact Assessment (PIA) (OAIC PIA Guide, p.3):

1. **APP 3 (Collection of Sensitive Information):** Performance notes and meeting transcripts may contain sensitive information (e.g., health status, racial or ethnic origin). Under APP 3.3, the collection of sensitive information generally requires the individual's explicit consent unless a specific exception applies (APP Guidelines, p.91).
2. **APP 6 (Secondary Use):** If personal information or feedback logs are used to train, fine-tune, or test the AI model, and this was not the primary purpose of collection, the agency must establish that this secondary use is within the reasonable expectations of the individuals or obtain explicit consent (OAIC AI Training Top 5).
3. **APP 11 (Security of Personal Information):** Given the mobile-first deployment and integration with internal databases, robust security measures must be established to protect against unauthorised access, modification, or disclosure of sensitive employee records (OAIC AI Selection Checklist, p.2; Threshold Assessment §3.7).

Consequently, compliance cannot be confirmed until a comprehensive PIA is completed and its recommendations are integrated into the system design.

*Citations: [OAIC PIA Guide, p.3], [APP Guidelines, p.91], [OAIC AI Training Top 5, p.1], [OAIC AI Selection Checklist, p.2]*

## 7.2

The preliminary Privacy Threshold Assessment is recorded and maintained within this AI Impact Assessment workflow (WT-BT35-S6). As recommended in the threshold assessment (Threshold Assessment §4.1), a formal Privacy Impact Assessment (PIA) is required due to the high-risk handling of sensitive employee performance data and transcripts. Once the PIA is initiated and completed, both the threshold assessment and the final PIA report will be stored in the agency's official electronic document management system (e.g., Content Manager / TRIM). Keeping a permanent record of the threshold assessment and subsequent PIA is a key accountability requirement under OAIC guidelines (OAIC PIA Guide, p.9).
