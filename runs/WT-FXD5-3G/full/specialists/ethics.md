## 5.1

No. The project outline does not indicate that a formal definition of fairness has been established for the ATO Deduction Assistant. However, the threshold assessment (Section 3.2) identifies key fairness risks, particularly that the system might provide inconsistent guidance for non-standard employment types (such as gig economy workers or sole traders) compared to standard wage earners. To align with Australia's AI Ethics Principles and the Voluntary AI Safety Standard (VAISS), the ATO must explicitly define and document what constitutes a fair process and outcome, ensuring the system is inclusive and accessible and does not result in unfair discrimination (AI Ethics Principles, §AI Ethics Principles at a glance; VAISS, s 10 [voluntary-ai-safety-standard:00019]).

*Citations: [AI Ethics Principles, §AI Ethics Principles at a glance], [VAISS, s 10]*

## 5.2

No. There is currently no documented methodology for measuring the fairness of the chatbot's outcomes quantitatively or qualitatively. The success criteria in the outline focus on inquiry call volume, click-through rates, and general sentiment scores, but do not include specific metrics for fairness or bias detection. To manage the identified risks of algorithmic bias and inconsistent guidance (Threshold Assessment, Section 3.2), the ATO should establish clear, measurable acceptance criteria and evaluation metrics to identify and mitigate unwanted bias or discriminatory outputs, particularly for marginalised or non-standard taxpayer groups (VAISS, s 10 [voluntary-ai-safety-standard:00019]; Diversity & Inclusion in AI, p.16 [2305-12728v1:00021]).

*Citations: [VAISS, s 10], [Diversity & Inclusion in AI, p.16]*

## 8.1

No. While the threshold assessment (Section 2.4) identifies primary users (individual taxpayers) and internal stakeholders (ATO support staff and policy teams), the project outline does not indicate that active consultation has yet taken place with these groups. Under VAISS Guardrail 10, it is critical to engage with stakeholders throughout the AI lifecycle, particularly at the earliest stages, to identify potential harms and evaluate their needs, circumstances, and experiences (VAISS, s 10 [voluntary-ai-safety-standard:00018]). The ATO should establish structured consultation processes to gather feedback from these stakeholder groups before deployment.

*Citations: [VAISS, s 10]*

## 8.2

Yes. The chatbot will be hosted publicly on the ATO portal, and the user interface will feature mandatory disclaimers, direct links to official policy documentation for verification, and a 'creativity' dial serving as a meta-signal for system fallibility (Outline, Section 7). This aligns with transparency principles by disclosing the system's advisory nature and limitations. To fully satisfy transparency and accountability standards, the ATO should also maintain a public record or document the system's scope, capabilities, limitations, and expected contexts of use (VAISS, s 10 [voluntary-ai-safety-standard:00018]; AI Ethics Principles, §AI Ethics Principles at a glance [australia-s-ai-ethics-principles:00001]).

*Citations: [VAISS, s 10], [AI Ethics Principles, §AI Ethics Principles at a glance]*

## 8.4

Yes. 

8.4.1: Yes, users will be clearly informed when they are interacting with an AI system. The web-based chat interface integrated into the ATO portal will feature prominent, mandatory disclaimers and a 'creativity' dial that acts as a meta-signal for system fallibility (Outline, Section 7), ensuring transparent disclosure of the AI's role (VAISS, s 10 [voluntary-ai-safety-standard:00015]).

8.4.2: Yes, members of the public interacting with the system will be provided with the ability to request a non-AI alternative. The outline notes that taxpayers can use existing non-AI alternatives such as static FAQ wizards, interactive decision trees, or direct contact with ATO support staff (Outline, Sections 3, 6), ensuring that users are not forced to rely solely on the AI chatbot for guidance.

*Citations: [VAISS, s 10]*

## 8.5

Yes. The chatbot is designed to provide guidance based on specific tax policy documents and will cite the exact source of its information, directing users to the official ATO website for final verification (Outline, Section 5). This RAG-based citation mechanism allows users to understand the basis of the chatbot's recommendations and verify the underlying factors, supporting the principle of transparency and explainability (AI Ethics Principles, §AI Ethics Principles at a glance [australia-s-ai-ethics-principles:00001]; VAISS, s 10 [voluntary-ai-safety-standard:00015]).

*Citations: [AI Ethics Principles, §AI Ethics Principles at a glance], [VAISS, s 10]*
