### 3.1 Reducing service accessibility and inclusion

The outline does not specify the user interface or accessibility features. Precautionarily, if agricultural stakeholders rely on this system for tracking, regional or remote farming communities may face accessibility barriers due to limited connectivity or digital literacy, leading to moderate consequences.

### 3.2 Unfair discrimination

The outline is silent on the data sources and algorithms used. Precautionarily, there is a risk that the AI model could unfairly discriminate against smaller-scale, organic, or indigenous farming operations by misclassifying their practices compared to large-scale industrial baselines, potentially leading to unfair resource allocation or disproportionate scrutiny.

### 3.3 Stereotyping or demeaning representations

While the system primarily tracks livestock data, there is a minor risk that profiling agricultural practices or regional performance could indirectly perpetuate negative stereotypes about specific regional communities or farming sectors.

### 3.4 Harm

Erroneous AI analysis of livestock health (such as false negatives on contagious diseases or false positives leading to unnecessary quarantine or culling) could cause noticeable financial and operational harm to agricultural businesses, as well as ecological harm to regional biosecurity.

### 3.5 Privacy concerns

The outline does not specify what data is collected. Precautionarily, tracking large-scale agricultural data likely involves commercial-in-confidence business details, geospatial data, and personal information of landholders, raising moderate privacy concerns if mishandled.

### 3.6 Security concerns — data aspects

Large-scale agricultural and biosecurity data may be sensitive and targeted by malicious actors or competitors. Without specified security measures, unauthorized access to regional livestock health trends could pose biosecurity or economic risks, assuming a moderate data compromise is possible.

### 3.7 Security concerns — system aspects

The technical implementation details, system architecture, and integration methods are not stated in the outline. Precautionarily, introducing an AI-driven system into public department infrastructure presents potential software vulnerabilities or integration flaws that could lead to system-level security issues.

### 3.8 Reputation or public confidence

If the AI system fails to accurately monitor livestock health, misses a major disease outbreak, or falsely flags healthy livestock, it would lead to public questioning of the department's oversight and a moderate loss of confidence in agricultural management.

## 4. Threshold assessment outcome

### 4.1 Assessing officer recommendation

This threshold assessment has been conducted under a highly precautionary posture due to the very limited information available in the current project outline. The proposed Livestock Data Management System aims to address critical delays in agricultural health monitoring, but the lack of technical, data, and architectural details introduces several potential risks.

Key areas of concern include potential biosecurity and economic impacts if the AI system fails to accurately monitor livestock health (such as missed outbreaks or unnecessary culling), as well as privacy risks associated with tracking geospatial and commercial-in-confidence agricultural data. Furthermore, accessibility barriers for rural or remote farmers must be carefully evaluated once the user interface is designed.

It is recommended that the project team thoroughly document the system's data sources, algorithmic models, user interface designs, and security controls as they are developed. A comprehensive risk management plan should be established to address potential biases, data security vulnerabilities, and contingency protocols for system failures to ensure public and industry confidence is maintained.

A full assessment is **required**.
